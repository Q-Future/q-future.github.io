Q-Future Workshop Proposal @ ECCV2024

Can we build visual evaluation systems with/for large-scale generative models (LGMs)?

Challenge

Challenge Tracks

The general focus of the challenges is to measure how emerging visual evaluation systems can precisely answer open-ended questions related to the corresponding 4 track scopes. To ensure more objective quantitative evaluation among participants, all track questions are multi-choice questions (MCQ) with multiple candidate options provided, and final metrics will be based on the accuracy on answering the MCQs. The tracks are as follows:

Answering quality-related questions for single images.
Answering quality-related questions comparing multiple images.
Answering aesthetic-related questions for single images.
Answering alignment questions between text and generated image.

Datasets

Datasets for each challenge track can be accessed on their respective Codalab sites. All phase submissions should be made via the Codalab website. Participants are required to register on the site and follow the provided instructions for downloading datasets and submitting their results. The specific evaluation metrics for each track will be detailed on the relevant site.

Proceeding Papers

Call for Papers

We welcome proceeding paper submission that are related to new trends provided, or new questions raised, by the powerful large-scale generative models in the domain of visual evaluation:
  • Can we develop better visual evaluation systems with LGM? This includes using MLLMs (or other generative models) to develop novel text-interactive visual evaluation systems, or utilizing these large generative models to improve the accuracy for conventional visual evaluation.
  • Can we develop better visual evaluation systems for LGM? This includes not only more robust quality or aesthetic evaluators on AI-generated contents (AIGC), but also more advanced systems that can comprehensively examine whether the generated content follows the prompt of its creator.
We highly encourage top-performance challenge participants to submit a paper to the workshop proceedings.

Research@Q-Future

The Q-Future team has been a pioneer on large-scale generative models (LGM) for visual evaluation. The team has published/released several fundamental studies on this domain:

  • Q-Bench, the first benchmark for MLLMs on low-level vision and visual evaluation. It is accepted as a spotlight paper to ICLR2024.
  • Q-Instruct, the first large-scale dataset to improve low-level visual abilities and IQA abilities of MLLMs, with the first family of low-level visual assistants.
  • Q-Align, the state-of-the-art visual scorer with MLLMs, with best performance of quality and aesthetic evaluation in one model.
